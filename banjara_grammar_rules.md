# Banjara Grammar & Sentence Structure Analysis

This document provides a detailed breakdown of Banjara sentences, mapping English words to Banjara and analyzing the grammatical structure.

**General Rule:** Banjara, like many Indian languages, typically follows the **Subject-Object-Verb (SOV)** order, whereas English follows Subject-Verb-Object (SVO).

### Key Grammar Findings
1.  **Sentence Order**: Banjara strictly follows **(Subject) - (Object) - (Verb)**.
    *   *English*: I (S) eat (V) apple (O).
    *   *Banjara*: Hu (S) safarjan (O) khau chu (V).
2.  **Postpositions**: Instead of prepositions (in, on, to), Banjara uses suffixes attached to the noun.
    *   *To* the market -> Dukaan-**en**
    *   *In* the stomach -> Peet-**emai**
    *   *Of* the shirt -> Jhigla-**re**
3.  **Verb Endings**: Common verb endings include:
    *   `che`, `chu`, `cha` = is/am/are (Present Tense)
    *   `koni`, `cheeni` = not / don't have (Negation)
